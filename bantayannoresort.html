Mangrove Eco Park

A nice two-hour adventure on Bantayan Island is the Obo-ob Mangrove Eco Park. The eco park has a bamboo walkway through the mangroves which makes for a refreshing stroll with good photo opportunities.

Ogtong Cave

Ogtong Cave is privately owned by a resort of the same name, so it’s not exactly a hidden gem, but it’s well worth visiting if you’re looking for a unique Bantayan Island tourist spot.

St. Peter and Paul Parish

The most imposing historical structure in Bantayan is the over-a-century old Sts. Peter and Paul Parish Church. Located at the heart of the town, just across the town plaza, the church is made of coral stones put together by a mixture of tree sap and limestone.
